A complex regulatory & systems problem

Local Laws Hub

Making complex regulatory compliance clear, actionable, and iterable for hosts — a modular system paired with a real-time feedback loop.

Jeff Alford · Lead UX & Product Designer01
Overview~1 min

One central hub for compliance

A component-by-component modular page built for maximum flexibility across thousands of jurisdictions — so every host sees exactly what rules apply to their property and can act on them.

Challenge
Hosts face ever-changing city, county, and state short-term rental laws. Compliance info was scattered or missing — causing confusion, support tickets, and listing takedowns.
Goal
Build a single source of truth where every host sees exactly what rules apply to their listing and can take action immediately.
Scope
Registration, Booking Limits, Taxes, Jurisdictions, Legal Acknowledgments, Get Involved, and Feedback.
Jeff Alford · Lead UX & Product Designer02
The Problem~1 min

Hosts were lost in a sea of laws and taxes

“I didn’t know my city requires a 30-night minimum.”

“Why is Vrbo blocking my calendar?”

“Taxes are confusing — am I collecting them or not?”— Host feedback

Resulting in: frustrated hosts, increased support tickets, compliance risk, and lost revenue.

Jeff Alford · Lead UX & Product Designer03
Strategy~2 min

Modular and scalable: build once, deploy anywhere

  • Component-first architecture: Booking Limits, Taxes, Registration, Legal — each built as an independent, reusable module.
  • Progressive disclosure: only show what matters; contextual help and expandable sections reduce cognitive load.
  • Jurisdiction-aware: dynamic content that flexes for thousands of cities, counties, and states.
Jeff Alford · Lead UX & Product Designer04
Key Feature~2 min

Booking Limits

The most complex component we solved. Shortly after launch, our feedback tools surfaced confusion around the booking limits component.

Within a few weeks we updated the component based on that feedback — and users immediately stopped feeling the need to comment on this section.

How we listened: Opinion Lab, Slack integration, and session-replay recordings.

Jeff Alford · Lead UX & Product Designer05
Key Feature~1 min

Taxes & Registration

Multiple tax options paired with smart registration flows that adapt to each property’s jurisdiction.

  • Tax collection: clear states for “collect & remit” vs. “collect & send to me,” with itemized authority, rate, and tax type.
  • Registration flows: guided entry, expiration tracking, and warning states when details need updating.
Jeff Alford · Lead UX & Product Designer06
Key Feature~1 min

Legal Acknowledgments & Jurisdictions

Jurisdiction context (country, state, county, city, plus local notices) paired with required legal acknowledgments.

  • Jurisdiction map: shows exactly which authorities apply, with a link to the local jurisdiction’s website.
  • Acknowledgments: government-required reading with progressive disclosure (“show all”) and a clear acknowledged state.
Jeff Alford · Lead UX & Product Designer07
Our Secret Sauce~1 min

An integrated feedback loop, built into the MVP

“Quickly proved itself within the first few weeks of launch — allowing us to iterate, improve, and remove confusion for the booking limits component.”

On-page feedback was paired with Slack integration and session-replay recordings, letting us turn signal into iteration in days, not quarters.
